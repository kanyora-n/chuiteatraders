Understanding Kenyan Tea Grades

We trade in primary CTC (Crush, Tear, Curl) grades, ensuring quality and consistency for our clients worldwide.

BP1 (Broken Pekoe 1)

This grade forms about 12-14% of total production. It has a larger particle size and produces a flavourful tea with a bright, coppery liquor.

PF1 (Pekoe Fannings 1)

Making up about 58-60% of production, this is the ideal grade for tea bags. It infuses quickly, producing a brisk, strong, and colorful cup.

PD (Pekoe Dust)

This grade is characterized by its fine, black, and granular particles. It produces a very strong and robust brew with a deep red color.

D1 (Dust 1)

Made up of the smallest particles, D1 is sought after for its rapid infusion and very strong liquor, ideal for blends requiring a powerful base.

The Art and History of Tea

Tea is more than just a beverage; it's a culture, a ritual, and a source of wellness. Kenyan tea, in particular, is globally renowned for its quality. Grown in the pristine high altitudes of the Great Rift Valley, the unique climate gives our tea its signature robust flavor and beautiful amber hue.

We are proud to be part of this tradition, offering exceptional black, green, and purple teas that tell the story of our rich Kenyan heritage.
